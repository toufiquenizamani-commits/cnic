Pakistan National Identity Card Structure

What do the first 5 digits represent?

The first 5 digits represents the location of the cardholder. The first digit represents the province, second represents division, third represents district, fourth represents tehsil, and the fifth represents the Union Council.

What is the purpose of the middle 7 digits?

The middle 7 digits are a family tracking number. NADRA groups members of the same family together to compile relationship family trees for verify purposes.

What does the final 1 digit tell us?

The last check digit denotes gender. Odd values (1, 3, 5, 7, 9) are assigned to male cardholders, while even values (2, 4, 6, 8) are assigned to female cardholders.

Is my date of birth hidden in the card number?

No. The CNIC digits contain no hidden dates, age, or expiry configurations. All timestamps are stored and cross-referenced in the secure databases of NADRA.
